Maritime Port Cyber Policy
#+begin_quote
33 CFR Part 101, Subpart F establishes the mandatory baseline cybersecurity standards for
U.S.-flagged vessels, Outer Continental Shelf (OCS) facilities, and maritime facilities regulated
under the Maritime Transportation Security Act (MTSA). It mandates strict requirements for incident
reporting, system protections, and personnel oversight to secure the Marine Transportation System
(MTS).

Core mandates outlined in Subpart F (33 CFR 101.600 - 615) encompass:

Cybersecurity Plans: Affected owners and operators must develop and maintain documented
Cybersecurity Plans and Cyber Incident Response Plans.

Data Security Measures: These plans require secure capturing and storage of digital logs, alongside
effective encryption to maintain the confidentiality and integrity of critical IT and Operational
Technology (OT) traffic.

Designated Personnel: Entities must designate a Cybersecurity Officer (CySO) charged with
implementing the security plans, organizing annual audits, and overseeing cybersecurity training.

Incident Reporting: Operators must immediately report "reportable cyber incidents" (events that
significantly disrupt business operations, lead to unauthorized access, or cause a transportation
security incident) to the National Response Center (NRC).
#+end_quote
